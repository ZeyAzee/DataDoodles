Project Overview

This project explores how gender inequality still shapes women’s economic opportunities around the world. Using World Bank Gender Statistics from 2010 to 2024, we compare men and women in employment, labor participation, and political representation. Through data and visual storytelling, we highlight both the progress made and the gaps that remain in achieving true equality.

Data Sources & Methodology

Primary Sources

  • World Bank – Gender Statistics

  • International Labour Organization (ILO) – Employment & Labor data

  • World Development Indicators (WDI) – Women in Parliament

Data Characteristics

  • Time Period: 2010–2024

  • Geographic Coverage: 16 Countries across 6 regions

  • Indicators: Employment, Labor Force Participation, Women in Parliament

  • Data Points: ~1,200 after filtering and cleaning

Data Filters

The dataset spans 2010–2024 and includes 16 countries selected to represent diverse regions and economic conditions worldwide:

  • Europe (Germany, Italy, Spain, Sweden, Norway, Belarus)

  • Asia (China, India, Iran, Japan)

  • Africa (Kenya, South Africa)

  • North America (Canada)

  • South America (Argentina, Brazil)

  • Oceania (Australia)

Five indicators from the World Bank Gender Statistics — employment rates, labor force participation, and women’s representation in parliament — were chosen to offer a balanced, global perspective on gender inequality.
